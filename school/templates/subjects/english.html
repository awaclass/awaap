English Language Syllabus

JAMB UTME 2026 · Use of English · 60 Questions

Exam Structure: Section A — Comprehension (5Q) + Cloze (10Q) + Reading Text (10Q)  |  Section B — Sentence Interpretation (5Q) + Antonyms (5Q) + Synonyms (5Q) + Sentence Completion (10Q)  |  Section C — Oral Forms (10Q)  ·  Total: 60 Questions
1 Comprehension Passages
🎯 Objectives: Identify main points/topic sentences; determine implied meaning; identify grammatical functions of words, phrases, clauses and figurative/idiomatic expressions; deduce the writer's intentions including mood, attitude and opinion.
  • Description passages
  • Narration passages
  • Exposition passages
  • Argumentation/Persuasion passages
  • Two passages per exam (one is a cloze test), each ~200 words
2 Comprehension Skills Tested
🎯 Objectives: Test whole-passage and part-passage understanding including word meaning, logical reasoning and synthesis of ideas.
  • Comprehension of whole or part of each passage
  • Comprehension of words, phrases, clauses, sentences, figures of speech and idioms
  • Coherence and logical reasoning (deductions, inferences, etc.)
  • Synthesis of ideas — combining distinct pieces of information into a complete whole
3 Reading Text — The Lekki Headmaster
🎯 Objectives: Demonstrate in-depth comprehension of the prescribed reading text for 2026.
  • The Lekki Headmaster by Kabir Alabi Garba — 10 questions in the exam
  • Plot, character, themes, setting and literary devices
  • Author's style, tone and message
1 Vocabulary — Synonyms, Antonyms & Homonyms
🎯 Objectives: Identify words and expressions in their ordinary, figurative and idiomatic contexts; determine similar and opposite meanings of words.
  • Synonyms — words of similar meaning
  • Antonyms — words of opposite meaning
  • Homonyms — words with same spelling/sound but different meanings
  • Ordinary usage, figurative usage and idiomatic usage
  • Idioms tested are formal and expressed in standard British English
2 Grammar — Clause & Sentence Patterns
🎯 Objectives: Identify various grammatical patterns in use; interpret information conveyed in sentences.
  • Clause and sentence patterns
  • Word classes and their functions
  • Mood, tense and aspect
  • Number, agreement/concord
  • Degree — positive, comparative and superlative
  • Question tags
3 Punctuation & Spelling
🎯 Objectives: Differentiate between correct and incorrect punctuation and spelling.
  • Punctuation marks and their correct use
  • Common spelling errors and correct forms
1 Vowels
🎯 Objectives: Make distinctions between vowel types.
  • Monophthongs (pure vowels)
  • Diphthongs (gliding vowels)
2 Consonants
🎯 Objectives: Differentiate between consonant types.
  • Individual consonant sounds
  • Consonant clusters
3 Rhymes & Homophones
🎯 Objectives: Identify words that rhyme and words that sound the same but differ in meaning or spelling.
  • Rhyming words
  • Homophones (e.g. hear/here, bare/bear)
4 Word Stress
🎯 Objectives: Identify correct accentuation in individual words and connected speech.
  • Monosyllabic word stress
  • Polysyllabic word stress
5 Intonation & Emphatic Stress
🎯 Objectives: Identify correct accentuation in connected speech and understand how stress placement changes meaning.
  • Intonation patterns in utterances
  • Emphatic stress — placement of normal stress on words for emphasis
