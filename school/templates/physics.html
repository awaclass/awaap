Physics Syllabus

JAMB UTME 2026 · 39 Topic Areas

1Measurements and Units
🎯 Objectives: Use measuring instruments; determine dimensions; estimate errors; plot graphs.
  • Length, area, volume — metre rule, Vernier calipers, micrometer, measuring cylinder
  • Mass, time, fundamental & derived quantities
  • Dimensions, homogeneity of equations, estimation of errors, significant figures
2Scalars and Vectors
🎯 Objectives: Distinguish scalars/vectors; resolve vectors; relative velocity.
  • Definition and examples, relative velocity, resolution into components, graphical methods
3Motion
🎯 Objectives: Linear motion, projectiles, Newton's laws, circular motion, SHM.
  • Types: translational, oscillatory, rotational
  • Linear motion equations, motion under gravity, graphs
  • Projectiles (range, max height, time of flight)
  • Newton's laws, impulse, momentum, conservation
  • Circular motion (angular velocity, centripetal force)
  • SHM: period, frequency, energy, resonance
4Gravitational Field
🎯 Objectives: Newton's law, gravitational potential, escape velocity, weightlessness.
  • Universal gravitation, gravitational potential, g variation, mass vs weight, escape velocity, parking orbit
5Equilibrium of Forces
🎯 Objectives: Conditions for equilibrium, moments, Lami's theorem, centre of gravity.
  • Equilibrium of particles, triangle/polygon of forces, Lami's theorem
  • Moments, couples, rigid body equilibrium, centre of gravity (stable/unstable/neutral)
6Pressure
🎯 Objectives: S.I. units, pressure instruments, P = ρgh, Pascal's principle.
  • Atmospheric pressure, barometers, manometers, variation with height, Pascal's principle applications
7Liquids at Rest
🎯 Objectives: Density, upthrust, Archimedes' principle, law of floatation.
  • Density, relative density, upthrust, Archimedes' principle, applications (ships, hydrometers)
8Temperature & Its Measurement
🎯 Objectives: Thermometric properties, scales, Celsius/Kelvin conversion.
  • Concept of temperature, calibration, Celsius & Kelvin, types of thermometers
9Thermal Expansion
🎯 Objectives: Linear/volume expansivities, anomalous expansion of water.
  • Solids: linear, area, volume expansivities; liquids: real/apparent expansivity, anomalous expansion
10Gas Laws
🎯 Objectives: Boyle's, Charles', pressure law, ideal gas equation, Van der Waals.
  • Boyle, Charles, Pressure law, absolute zero, general gas equation, PV=nRT, Van der Waals
11Quantity of Heat
🎯 Objectives: Heat capacity, specific heat capacity, methods of mixtures.
  • Heat capacity, specific heat capacity, method of mixtures, electrical method, Newton's law of cooling
12Change of State
🎯 Objectives: Latent heats, melting, evaporation, boiling, pressure effects.
  • Latent heat of fusion/vaporisation, melting/evaporation/boiling distinctions, pressure & dissolved substances effects
13Vapours
🎯 Objectives: Saturated/unsaturated vapours, SVP, dew point, humidity.
  • SVP, relation to boiling, dew point, relative humidity, hygrometers
14Heat Transfer
🎯 Objectives: Conduction, convection, radiation, thermal conductivity, engines.
  • Modes: conduction, convection, radiation; thermal conductivity, thermos flask, land/sea breeze, engines
15Structure of Matter & Kinetic Theory
🎯 Objectives: Molecular theory, Brownian motion, diffusion, surface tension, kinetic theory assumptions.
  • Atoms, molecules, Brownian motion, diffusion, surface tension, capillarity, adhesion/cohesion, kinetic theory
16Waves
🎯 Objectives: Wave motion, V=fλ, progressive equation, classification, properties (reflection, refraction, diffraction, polarisation, superposition, beats, Doppler).
  • Production & propagation, V=fλ, phase difference, progressive wave equation Y = A sin 2π/λ (vt ± x)
  • Mechanical/EM, longitudinal/transverse, stationary/progressive
  • Reflection, refraction, diffraction, polarisation, interference, beats, Doppler effect
17Propagation of Sound Waves
🎯 Objectives: Need for medium, speed of sound in solids/liquids/air, echoes, reverberation.
  • Material medium necessity, speed in different media, effect of temp/pressure, echoes, reverberation
18Characteristics of Sound Waves
🎯 Objectives: Noise vs musical notes, quality/pitch/intensity, overtones, resonance in pipes.
  • Noise vs musical notes, quality, pitch, intensity, loudness, overtones (strings & air columns), resonance, frequencies in closed/open pipes
19Light Energy
🎯 Objectives: Compare natural/artificial sources; luminous/non-luminous; speed/freq/wavelength; shadows, eclipses; pin-hole camera.
  • Sources: natural & artificial, luminous & non-luminous objects
  • Propagation: speed, frequency, wavelength; shadows & eclipse formation
  • Pin-hole camera principle and problems
20Reflection at Plane & Curved Surfaces
🎯 Objectives: Laws of reflection; images by plane, concave, convex mirrors; mirror formula 1/f = 1/u + 1/v; linear magnification; applications (periscope, kaleidoscope, sextant).
  • Laws of reflection, image formation (plane, concave, convex), ray diagrams
  • Mirror formula, linear magnification, applications
21Refraction Through Plane & Curved Surfaces
🎯 Objectives: Snell's law, refractive index, real & apparent depth, critical angle, TIR, prism formula n = sin[(A+D)/2] / sin(A/2), lens formula 1/f = 1/u + 1/v, Newton's formula, magnification.
  • Refraction (velocity change), laws, refractive index (Snell's law), real/apparent depth, lateral displacement
  • Critical angle & total internal reflection (TIR), applications (mirage, optical fibre)
  • Glass prism: minimum deviation formula, types of lenses, lens formula, Newton's formula, magnification
22Optical Instruments
🎯 Objectives: Microscope, telescope, projector, camera, human eye; power of lens, angular magnification, near/far points, sight defects & corrections.
  • Principles of microscopes, telescopes, projectors, cameras, human eye (no physiological details)
  • Power of lens (P = 1/f), angular magnification, near & far points
  • Defects: myopia, hypermetropia, astigmatism & corrections
23Dispersion & Electromagnetic Spectrum
🎯 Objectives: Dispersion by prism, pure spectrum, colour mixing (additive/subtractive), colour filters, rainbow; EM spectrum sources & uses.
  • Dispersion of white light, pure spectrum production, primary/secondary colours, colour of objects & filters, rainbow formation
  • EM spectrum: radio, microwave, IR, visible, UV, X-rays, gamma rays — sources, detection, applications
24Electrostatics
🎯 Objectives: Charges, charging methods, electroscope, Coulomb's law, electric field, potential, lightning conductors.
  • Positive/negative charges, charging (friction, contact, induction), electroscope, Coulomb's inverse square law, E field & potential, discharge, lightning
25Capacitors
🎯 Objectives: Types, parallel plate, C = εA/d, series/parallel combinations, energy stored.
  • Types & functions, parallel plate capacitor, capacitance, factors affecting C (area, separation, dielectric), series/parallel, energy stored (½CV²)
26Electric Cells
🎯 Objectives: Simple voltaic cell defects, Daniel, Leclanché, accumulators, arrangement of cells, efficiency.
  • Simple voltaic cell defects & correction, Daniel, Leclanché (wet/dry), lead-acid, NiFe, Li-ion, maintenance, series/parallel arrangement, efficiency
27Current Electricity
🎯 Objectives: emf, p.d., current, internal resistance, Ohm's law, meter bridge, potentiometer, Kirchhoff's laws.
  • emf, p.d., current, internal resistance, lost volt; Ohm's law; resistance measurement, meter bridge; series/parallel combinations; potentiometer (emf, current, internal resistance); Kirchhoff's laws
28Electrical Energy & Power
🎯 Objectives: Energy, power, commercial unit, transmission, heating effect, house wiring, fuses.
  • Energy & power expressions, commercial unit (kWh), power transmission, heating effect, house wiring (parallel advantages), fuse rating
29Magnets & Magnetic Fields
🎯 Objectives: Natural/artificial magnets, soft iron vs steel, making/demagnetizing, field patterns, Earth's magnetic field, flux, navigation.
  • Natural/artificial magnets, magnetic properties of soft iron/steel, making & demagnetizing methods, field patterns (permanent, straight wire, solenoid), Earth's field (dip, declination), flux & flux density, applications (navigation, mineral exploration)
30Force on Conductor in Magnetic Field
🎯 Objectives: Fleming's left-hand rule, force between conductors, charge in B-field, DC motor, galvanometer conversion.
  • Force on current-carrying conductor, force between parallel conductors, force on moving charge, DC motor, electromagnets, moving coil/iron instruments, galvanometer to ammeter/voltmeter (shunt/multiplier), sensitivity
31Electromagnetic Induction
🎯 Objectives: Faraday's laws, Lenz's law, AC/DC generators, transformers, inductance, eddy currents.
  • Faraday's laws, factors affecting induced emf, Lenz's law (energy conservation), AC/DC generators, transformers, induction coil, inductance (unit, energy stored = ½LI²), eddy currents (reduction & applications)
32Simple AC Circuits
🎯 Objectives: AC vs DC, peak/rms values, R, C, L circuits, RC, RL, RLC series, phasors, resonance f₀ = 1/(2π√LC), power factor.
  • AC current/voltage, peak & rms, resistor in AC, capacitive reactance, inductive reactance, series RLC, vector diagrams, phase angle, impedance, resonance frequency, instantaneous/average power, power factor
33Conduction Through Liquids & Gases
🎯 Objectives: Electrolytes vs non-electrolytes, electrolysis, Faraday's laws, discharge through gases.
  • Liquids: electrolytes, electrolysis, Faraday's laws, applications (electroplating, ammeter calibration)
  • Gases: discharge through gases (qualitative), applications
34Elementary Modern Physics
🎯 Objectives: Atomic models, energy levels, photoelectric effect (Einstein, stopping potential), X-rays, radioactivity (α,β,γ), half-life, decay constant, fusion/fission, mass-energy equivalence (ΔE=Δmc²), wave-particle duality, uncertainty principle.
  • Models of atom (limitations), atomic structure, energy levels & spectra, thermionic & photoelectric emission, Einstein's equation, stopping potential, X-ray production & properties
  • Radioactivity: α, β, γ rays, half-life, decay constant, isotopes
  • Nuclear energy: fusion, fission, binding energy, mass defect, Einstein's equation
  • Wave-particle duality, electron diffraction, uncertainty principle
35Introductory Electronics
🎯 Objectives: Metals/semiconductors/insulators (band gap), intrinsic/extrinsic, n-type/p-type, diodes (rectification), transistors (amplification).
  • Distinction: metals, semiconductors, insulators (band gap concept); intrinsic & extrinsic semiconductors; electron & hole carriers; n-type & p-type; diodes & transistors; rectification & amplification
