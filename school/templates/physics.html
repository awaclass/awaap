Physics Syllabus

JAMB UTME 2026 · 22 Topic Areas

1 Measurements and Units
🎯 Objectives: Use measuring instruments; determine lengths, areas and volumes; deduce units of derived quantities; determine dimensions; estimate errors; express measurements in standard form; use coordinate systems and plot graphs.
  • Length, area and volume — metre rule, Vernier calipers, micrometer screw-gauge, measuring cylinder
  • Mass — unit of mass, simple beam balance (e.g. Buchhart's balance)
  • Time — unit and time-measuring devices
  • Fundamental and derived physical quantities with their units
  • Dimensions — definition and simple examples; testing homogeneity of equations
  • Limitations of experimental measurements — accuracy, estimation of errors, significant figures, standard form
  • Displacement — distinction from distance; concept of position and coordinates; frame of reference
2 Scalars and Vectors
🎯 Objectives: Distinguish between scalar and vector quantities; determine resultants; find relative velocity; resolve vectors into perpendicular components; use graphical methods.
  • Definition and examples of scalar and vector quantities
  • Relative velocity
  • Resolution of vectors into two perpendicular directions
  • Graphical methods of vector solution
3 Motion
🎯 Objectives: Identify types of motion; solve problems on linear motion, projectiles, Newton's laws, circular motion and SHM; interpret graphs; analyse energy changes in SHM.
  • Types of motion — translational, oscillatory, rotational, spin, random
  • Linear motion — speed, velocity, acceleration; equations of uniformly accelerated motion; motion under gravity; distance-time and velocity-time graphs
  • Projectiles — range, maximum height, time of flight; applications
  • Newton's laws — inertia, mass, force; impulse and momentum; force-time graph; conservation of linear momentum
  • Circular motion — angular velocity, angular acceleration, centripetal and centrifugal forces
  • Simple Harmonic Motion (SHM) — definition, period, frequency, amplitude; velocity and acceleration; energy changes; forced vibration and resonance
4 Gravitational Field
🎯 Objectives: Apply Newton's law of universal gravitation; deduce gravitational potential; differentiate mass from weight; determine escape velocity; describe parking orbit and weightlessness.
  • Newton's law of universal gravitation
  • Gravitational potential
  • Conservative and non-conservative fields
  • Acceleration due to gravity (g) and its variation on the Earth's surface
  • Mass vs. weight; escape velocity
  • Parking orbit and weightlessness
5 Equilibrium of Forces
🎯 Objectives: Apply conditions for equilibrium of coplanar and rigid-body forces; use triangle/polygon of forces and Lami's theorem; determine moments, resultants and equilibrants; differentiate types of equilibrium.
  • Equilibrium of particles — coplanar forces; triangle and polygon of forces; Lami's theorem
  • Principles of moments — moment of a force; couple (torque) and applications
  • Equilibrium of rigid bodies — resolution and composition of forces; resultant and equilibrant
  • Centre of gravity — stable, unstable and neutral equilibrium
1 Pressure
🎯 Objectives: Recognise S.I. units of pressure (Pa); identify pressure-measuring instruments; relate pressure to height; use a barometer as an altimeter; apply Pascal's principle.
  • Atmospheric pressure — definition, S.I. units (Pa)
  • Pressure measurement — simple mercury barometer, aneroid barometer and manometer
  • Variation of atmospheric pressure with height; barometer as altimeter
  • Pressure in liquids — P = ρgh; transmission of pressure (Pascal's Principle) and applications
2 Liquids at Rest
🎯 Objectives: Distinguish density from relative density; determine upthrust; apply Archimedes' principle and the law of floatation.
  • Density of solids and liquids; relative density
  • Upthrust on a body immersed in a liquid
  • Archimedes' principle and law of floatation — applications (ships, hydrometers)
1 Temperature and Its Measurement
🎯 Objectives: Identify thermometric properties; calibrate thermometers; differentiate temperature scales; convert between Celsius and Kelvin.
  • Concept of temperature and thermometric properties
  • Calibration of thermometers
  • Temperature scales — Celsius and Kelvin; conversion between scales
  • Types of thermometers
2 Thermal Expansion
🎯 Objectives: Determine linear and volume expansivities; assess effects and applications; determine volume, apparent and real expansivities of liquids; analyse anomalous expansion of water.
  • Solids — linear, volume and area expansivities; effects (e.g. railway lines, building strips); relationships between expansivities
  • Liquids — volume expansivity; real and apparent expansivities; anomalous expansion of water
3 Gas Laws
🎯 Objectives: Interpret gas laws and use them to solve numerical problems; interpret the Van der Waals equation for a real gas.
  • Boyle's law (isothermal process)
  • Charles' law (isobaric process)
  • Pressure law (volumetric process)
  • Absolute zero of temperature
  • General gas equation: PV/T = constant
  • Ideal gas equation: PV = nRT
  • Van der Waals gas (real gas treatment)
4 Quantity of Heat
🎯 Objectives: Differentiate heat capacity from specific heat capacity; determine both using simple methods; solve numerical problems.
  • Heat as a form of energy
  • Heat capacity and specific heat capacity of solids and liquids
  • Determination by method of mixtures, electrical method and Newton's law of cooling
5 Change of State
🎯 Objectives: Differentiate latent heat types; distinguish melting, evaporation and boiling; examine effects of pressure and dissolved substances on boiling/melting points; solve numerical problems.
  • Latent heat; specific latent heats of fusion and vaporisation
  • Melting, evaporation and boiling — distinctions
  • Influence of pressure and dissolved substances on boiling and melting points
  • Applications in appliances
6 Vapours
🎯 Objectives: Distinguish saturated from unsaturated vapours; relate SVP to boiling point; determine SVP by barometer tube; differentiate dew point, humidity and relative humidity; estimate atmospheric humidity.
  • Unsaturated and saturated vapours
  • Saturated vapour pressure (SVP) and its relation to boiling; determination by barometer tube method
  • Formation of dew, mist, fog and rain
  • Dew point, humidity and relative humidity
  • Hygrometry — wet and dry bulb hygrometers
7 Heat Transfer
🎯 Objectives: Differentiate conduction, convection and radiation; solve problems on thermal conductivity and heat flux; compare conductivities; relate thermos flask components; differentiate land and sea breeze; analyse engine principles.
  • Conduction, convection and radiation — modes of heat transfer
  • Temperature gradient, thermal conductivity and heat flux
  • Effect of surface nature on energy radiated and absorbed
  • Conductivities of common materials
  • The thermos flask
  • Land and sea breeze
  • Engines — internal combustion, jet engines and rockets
1 Structure of Matter and Kinetic Theory
🎯 Objectives: Differentiate atoms and molecules; use molecular theory to explain Brownian motion, diffusion, surface tension, capillarity, adhesion, cohesion and contact angle; examine assumptions of kinetic theory and use it to explain gas behaviour.
  • Molecular nature of matter — atoms and molecules
  • Molecular theory — Brownian motion, diffusion, surface tension, capillarity, adhesion, cohesion, angles of contact
  • Kinetic theory — assumptions; explaining gas pressure, Boyle's law, Charles' law, melting, boiling, vaporisation, evaporation and temperature change
2 Waves
🎯 Objectives: Interpret wave motion; relate frequency, wavelength and wave velocity; compute wave parameters using progressive wave equation; distinguish wave types; differentiate wave properties; analyse superposition, beats and Doppler effect.
  • Production and propagation — wave motion; vibrating systems; waves as energy transfer; V = fλ; phase difference, wave number and wave vector; progressive wave equation Y = A sin 2π/λ (vt ± x)
  • Classification — mechanical vs. electromagnetic; longitudinal vs. transverse; stationary vs. progressive; examples from springs, ropes, stretched strings and ripple tanks
  • Properties — reflection, refraction, diffraction and plane polarisation; superposition and interference; beats and beat frequency; Doppler effect (qualitative)
3 Propagation of Sound Waves
🎯 Objectives: Determine the need for a material medium; compare speed of sound in solids, liquids and air; relate temperature and pressure to speed of sound; solve problems on echoes, reverberation and speed; compare advantages and disadvantages of echoes.
  • Necessity for a material medium in sound propagation
  • Speed of sound in solids, liquids and air; effect of temperature and pressure
  • Reflection of sound — echoes and reverberation; applications
  • Disadvantages (and advantages) of echoes and reverberation
4 Characteristics of Sound Waves
🎯 Objectives: Differentiate noise from musical notes; analyse quality, pitch, intensity and loudness; evaluate their application in musical instruments; identify overtones from vibrating strings and air columns; itemise acoustic examples of resonance; determine frequencies of notes in open and closed pipes.
  • Noise and musical notes — distinctions
  • Quality, pitch, intensity and loudness — definitions and applications in musical instruments
  • Overtones produced by vibrating strings and air columns: F₀ = (1/2L)√(T/μ), where μ = m/L
  • Acoustic examples of resonance
  • Frequency of notes emitted by air columns in closed and open pipes in relation to their lengths
