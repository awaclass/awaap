Mathematics Syllabus

JAMB UTME 2026 · Nigeria

1 Number Bases
🎯 Objectives: Perform four basic operations (×, +, −, ÷); convert one base to another.
  • Operations in different number bases from 2 to 10
  • Conversion from one base to another including fractional parts
2 Fractions, Decimals, Approximations & Percentages
🎯 Objectives: Perform basic operations on fractions and decimals; express to specified significant figures/decimal places; calculate simple interest, profit/loss percent, ratio, proportion and rate; solve problems involving shares and VAT.
  • Fractions and decimals
  • Significant figures and decimal places
  • Percentage errors
  • Simple interest; profit and loss percent
  • Ratio, proportion and rate
  • Shares and Value Added Tax (VAT)
3 Indices, Logarithms & Surds
🎯 Objectives: Apply laws of indices; establish the relationship between indices and logarithms; solve problems in different bases in logarithms; simplify and rationalize surds; perform basic operations on surds.
  • Laws of indices
  • Standard form (scientific notation)
  • Laws of logarithm
  • Logarithm of any positive number to a given base
  • Change of bases in logarithm and applications
  • Relationship between indices and logarithm
  • Surds — simplification, rationalization and basic operations
4 Sets
🎯 Objectives: Identify types of sets; solve problems involving cardinality; solve set problems using symbols; use Venn diagrams for not more than 3 sets.
  • Types of sets: empty, universal, complements, subsets, finite, infinite and disjoint sets
  • Algebra of sets — union, intersection, complement
  • Cardinality of sets
  • Venn diagrams and their applications (up to 3 sets)
1 Polynomials
🎯 Objectives: Find subject of formula; apply factor and remainder theorems; multiply and divide polynomials (degree ≤ 3); factorize by regrouping, difference of two squares, perfect squares and cubics; solve simultaneous equations (one linear, one quadratic); interpret graphs of polynomials including maxima and minima.
  • Change of subject of formula
  • Factor and remainder theorems
  • Factorisation of polynomials of degree not exceeding 3
  • Multiplication and division of polynomials
  • Roots of polynomials not exceeding degree 3
  • Simultaneous equations — one linear, one quadratic
  • Graphs of polynomials of degree not greater than 3
2 Variation
🎯 Objectives: Solve problems involving direct, inverse, joint and partial variations; solve problems on percentage increase and decrease in variation.
  • Direct variation
  • Inverse variation
  • Joint variation
  • Partial variation
  • Percentage increase and decrease
3 Inequalities
🎯 Objectives: Solve problems on linear and quadratic inequalities; interpret graphs of inequalities.
  • Analytical and graphical solutions of linear inequalities
  • Quadratic inequalities with integral roots only
4 Progression (AP & GP)
🎯 Objectives: Determine the nth term of a progression; compute the sum of AP and GP; find sum to infinity of a given GP.
  • nth term of a progression
  • Sum of Arithmetic Progression (AP)
  • Sum of Geometric Progression (GP) and sum to infinity
5 Binary Operations
🎯 Objectives: Solve problems involving closure, commutativity, associativity and distributivity; solve problems involving identity and inverse elements.
  • Properties of closure, commutativity, associativity and distributivity
  • Identity and inverse elements (simple cases only)
6 Matrices & Determinants
🎯 Objectives: Perform basic operations (×, +, −, ÷) on matrices; calculate determinants; compute inverses of 2×2 matrices.
  • Algebra of matrices not exceeding 3×3
  • Determinants of matrices not exceeding 3×3
  • Inverses of 2×2 matrices (excluding quadratic and higher degree equations)
1 Euclidean Geometry
🎯 Objectives: Identify various types of lines and angles; solve problems involving polygons; calculate angles using circle theorems; identify construction procedures of special angles (30°, 45°, 60°, 75°, 90°, etc.).
  • Properties of angles and lines
  • Polygons: triangles, quadrilaterals and general polygons
  • Circles: angle properties, cyclic quadrilaterals and intersecting chords
  • Construction of special angles
2 Mensuration
🎯 Objectives: Calculate perimeters and areas of triangles, quadrilaterals, circles and composites; find arc length, chord, perimeters and areas of sectors/segments; calculate total surface areas and volumes of cuboids, cylinders, cones, pyramids, prisms, spheres and composites; determine distance between two points on Earth's surface.
  • Lengths and areas of plane geometrical figures
  • Lengths of arcs and chords of a circle
  • Perimeters and areas of sectors and segments of circles
  • Surface areas and volumes of simple solids and composite figures
  • The earth as a sphere — longitudes and latitudes
3 Loci
🎯 Objectives: Identify and interpret loci relating to parallel lines, perpendicular bisectors, angle bisectors and circles.
  • Locus in 2 dimensions based on geometric principles relating to lines and curves
  • Loci for parallel lines, perpendicular bisectors, angle bisectors and circles
4 Coordinate Geometry
🎯 Objectives: Determine midpoint and gradient of a line segment; find distance between two points; identify conditions for parallelism and perpendicularity; find equation of a line in two-point form, point-slope form, slope-intercept form and general form.
  • Midpoint and gradient of a line segment
  • Distance between two points
  • Parallel and perpendicular lines
  • Equations of straight lines (all forms)
5 Trigonometry
🎯 Objectives: Calculate sin, cos and tan for angles −360° ≤ θ ≤ 360°; apply special angles (30°, 45°, 60°, 75°, 90°, 105°, 135°); solve problems on elevation, depression and bearings; apply trig formulae to find areas of triangles; solve problems involving sine and cosine graphs.
  • Trigonometrical ratios of angles (−360° to 360°)
  • Angles of elevation and depression
  • Bearings
  • Areas and solutions of triangles
  • Graphs of sine and cosine
  • Sine rule and cosine formula
1 Representation of Data
🎯 Objectives: Identify and interpret frequency distribution tables; interpret information on histogram, bar chart and pie chart.
  • Frequency distribution (grouped and ungrouped)
  • Histogram, bar chart and pie chart
2 Measures of Location
🎯 Objectives: i. Calculate the mean, mode and median of ungrouped and grouped data (simple cases only); ii. use ogive to find the median, quartiles and percentiles.
  • Mean, mode and median of ungrouped and grouped data (simple cases only)
  • Cumulative frequency
  • Use of ogive to find median, quartiles and percentiles
3 Measures of Dispersion
🎯 Objectives: Calculate the range, mean deviation, variance and standard deviation of ungrouped and grouped data.
  • Range
  • Mean deviation
  • Variance
  • Standard deviation
4 Permutation & Combination
🎯 Objectives: Solve simple problems involving permutation and combination.
  • Linear and circular arrangements
  • Arrangements involving repeated objects
5 Probability
🎯 Objectives: Solve simple problems in probability (including addition and multiplication).
  • Experimental probability (tossing of coin, throwing of a dice, etc.)
  • Addition and multiplication of probabilities (mutual and independent cases)
1 Differentiation
🎯 Objectives: Find the limit of a function; differentiate explicit algebraic and simple trigonometrical functions.
  • Limit of a function
  • Differentiation of explicit algebraic functions
  • Differentiation of simple trigonometrical functions (sin x, cos x)
2 Application of Differentiation
🎯 Objectives: Solve problems involving applications of rate of change, maxima and minima.
  • Rate of change
  • Maxima and minima
3 Integration
🎯 Objectives: Solve problems of integration involving algebraic and simple trigonometric functions; calculate area under the curve (simple cases only).
  • Integration of explicit algebraic functions
  • Integration of simple trigonometrical functions
  • Area under the curve (simple cases only)
